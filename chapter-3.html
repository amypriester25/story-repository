Chapter 3

The Fairy Godmother Appears

Cinderella hadn't moved in hours when she heard a voice from across the room. It was the faintest whisper but an unmistakable sound. Frightened, she whipped her head around, searching for where the noise came from.

“Don’t worry, dear, it’s only me,” the voice revealed itself, as a floating fairy moved out of the shadows.

Still afraid and uncertain, Cinderella couldn’t muster a single word.

“Oh I’m sorry, perhaps I forgot to introduce myself, I’m your fairy godmother.”

Cinderella could now see the fairy in all her glory, gold dust radiating off of her like she herself was a star.

“My….my what?” was all Cinderella could muster.

“Oh, you don’t remember me?” the fairy replied. “I’ve always been around, since you were little I’ve kept an eye on you. But I’m afraid I can only appear to you when you are in your most desperate hour. And this my dear,” she gestured to Cinderella’s ball gown, still sopping wet in the corner, “this is most definitely your most desperate hour. But have no fear, I am here to help. You are going to that ball one way or another, and you are going to show your awful stepmother and stepsisters what you’re truly made of, which is beauty dear, and grace. But even more importantly, you are going to waltz with the prince! And when he meets you he will fall in love.”

Cinderella, still sitting on the ground, looked up to her fairy godmother with wide eyes in disbelief.

“I’m afraid I don’t understand,” she said.

“No need to explain the logistics of it dear, we really must get going,” the fairy godmother replied, whisking her wand around. “First we absolutely must replace that dress. You must be wearing something utterly radiant when you meet the prince.”

With a few flicks of the wrist, the fairy godmother’s wand generated a ball of flashing golden light that grew and grew until Cinderella was sure it would consume them both. Just when she thought it couldn't get any brighter, the light ceased and what remained was the most beautiful ball gown Cinderella had ever seen. A stunning blue color, the gown was made of the most delicate tulle and the smoothest satin. In a flash of the fairy godmother’s wand, the gown was transferred to Cinderella’s body and of course, it fit perfectly. Cinderella couldn't help but twirl and glide around the room in her new ball gown. The fairy godmother stood and admired her, proud of her work.

“That’s not all we have to do darling, we have to find a way for you to get to the ball,” she said. The godmother and the girl went out into the garden looking for potential carriages. As the fairy godmother pondered what her approach would be, her eyes landed on a fat orange pumpkin in the corner.

“Hmmm,” she wondered, her lips pursed together. Once again, with merely the flick of her wrist the pumpkin was at once transformed into a beautiful carriage. But the godmother soon noticed a problem…the carriage had no horses to pull it or a driver to hold the reigns.

The fairy godmother let out a groan. “This may be difficult,” she said. “In order to create a living thing like horses or a coachman, I have must have a living thing to transform it. And it’s only us around, I’m not sure what to do.”

“Would you be able to use any living creature, regardless of size?” Cinderella pondered, “Say like a chipmunk? Or a squirrel? Or birds?”

“Why yes, yes that would work quite well,” the godmother replied.

“Well, I think I may have a solution for us then,” Cinderella smiled. She began to sing, walking around the garden. Very soon, Cinderella’s old friends came flocking. Birds, squirrels, chipmunks, even deer raced to the sound of her voice, delighted to hear their beloved friend singing again. Within minutes, Cinderell’s garden was full of animals, all dancing along to her tune.

“Oh my,” the fairy godmother was astonished. “Well, this will work quite well indeed.” She chose two beautiful deer to be transformed into horses and a chipmunk to be the coachman.

Now the carriage was fully functioning and Cinderella was ready to go to the ball. But as she started to get into the carriage she realized she was barefoot.

“Oh my,” the fairy godmother exclaimed. “Well that won’t do, you can’t waltz with the prince if you're not wearing any shoes. Hmm, what should we give you? Something delicate and light, it has to be comfortable too so it must fit you perfectly. But it also must be beautiful, it has to match the radiance of your dress.”

Once again, with the flick of her wrist, the fairy godmother made something beautiful appear, and this time it was the most stunning creation of all. There in the grass, where the fairy godmother had pointed her wand, was a glass slipper. It looked like it belonged in a museum, with ornate detailing and flawless design.

Cinderella was in awe, eyes widening at the sight of the shoe.

“I couldn't possibly,” she protested, but the fairy godmother helped her slip each foot into the shoe while she was in disbelief. With both glass slippers on, wearing the perfect dress, anda pumpkin carriage at her disposal, Cinderella thought she was all ready. She began to step into the carriage before her fairy godmother stopped her one last time.

“One more thing Cinderella,” she started. “All of this,” she gestured to the carriage, the coachman, and Cinderella’s dress and shoes, “will disappear at midnight. The carriage will be transformed back into a pumpkin, the coachman to a chipmunk, the horses to deer, and your old rags will appear in place of the dress. So be sure you are home by midnight to avoid anyone seeing.”

Cinderella nodded, she didn’t say it but she hoped none of this would ever go away. With help from her fairy godmother, she had gotten to live in a world where anything was possible, where bad things could be made beautiful.

“Alright,” she said, mustering a smile. “I’ll be home by midnight.”

The fairy godmother nodded, giving her one last smile.

“And Cinderella,” she started.

“Yes?”

“Don’t let evil people tell you who you are. You define who you are. People try to break beautiful things because they don't understand them, don’t let them break you,” and with that, the fairy godmother disappeared into the night.
